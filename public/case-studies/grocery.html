Grocery Delivery App — End-to-End Redesign

Duration: 5 weeks   |   Tools: Figma, FigJam, Hotjar

Overview

This case study covers a redesign of a grocery delivery mobile app (Instacart-style) to simplify search & checkout, increase repeat orders, and improve delivery tracking transparency.

Problem Statement

Users were experiencing friction finding items, saving multiple delivery addresses, and understanding delivery ETAs — resulting in higher cart abandonment (25%).

Research

  • 10 user interviews (current app users)
  • Session recordings analysis (Hotjar)
  • Competitive audit of 3 grocery apps

Key Insights

  1. Users prefer category browsing over search for weekly staples.
  2. Multiple address flows confused users — they expected defaults.
  3. Delivery ETA and driver status were unclear, causing support tickets.

Design Solutions

  • Introduced persistent bottom navigation with clear categories and a prominent search field.
  • Simplified address management into a single step during checkout with clear default selection.
  • Created an order-tracking timeline with ETA, driver photo and contact actions.

Results

After iterative tests (prototype with 12 participants):

  • Checkout completion improved from 75% → 88% (mock test)
  • Average time to find an item reduced by 30%
  • User satisfaction score increased from 6.9 → 8.5

What I Learned

Balancing search and browse for grocery shoppers is crucial. Microcopy and progressive disclosure reduce cognitive load.
